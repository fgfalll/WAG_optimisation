Breakthrough Physics

Mathematical Justification

The breakthrough time of CO₂ in an EOR process is a critical parameter. It can be estimated using various models, considering factors like reservoir heterogeneity, fluid mobilities, and injection rates.

One common approach involves correlating breakthrough time with the Dykstra-Parsons coefficient and mobility ratio. Another considers the effects of gravity override and viscous fingering.

The general form for breakthrough time ($$t_{BT}$$) can be complex, but often involves terms related to:

  • Pore volume injected ($$V_{inj}$$)
  • Mobility ratio ($$M$$)
  • Heterogeneity (e.g., Dykstra-Parsons coefficient $$V_{DP}$$)
  • Gravity number ($$N_g$$)

For example, a simplified model might look like:

$$t_{BT} \propto \frac{V_{pore}}{q_{inj}} \cdot f(M, V_{DP}, N_g)$$

Where $$f$$ is a function accounting for various physical phenomena.
