Peng-Robinson Equation of State

The Peng-Robinson equation of state is a cubic equation of state that relates pressure, temperature, and molar volume of a substance. It is given by:

$$P = \frac{RT}{V_m - b} - \frac{a(T)}{V_m(V_m + b) + b(V_m - b)}$$

Where:

  • $$P$$ is the pressure.
  • $$R$$ is the universal gas constant.
  • $$T$$ is the temperature.
  • $$V_m$$ is the molar volume.
  • $$a(T)$$ and $$b$$ are substance-specific parameters.

Soave-Redlich-Kwong Equation of State

The Soave-Redlich-Kwong equation of state is another cubic equation of state, similar to the Peng-Robinson equation. It is given by:

$$P = \frac{RT}{V_m - b} - \frac{a(T)}{V_m(V_m + b)}$$
