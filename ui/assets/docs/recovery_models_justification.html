Recovery Models

Buckley-Leverett Model

The Buckley-Leverett model describes the displacement of oil by an immiscible fluid (e.g., water or gas). The fractional flow of the displacing fluid is given by:

$$f_w = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{k_{ro}}{k_{rw}} \frac{\mu_w}{\mu_o}}$$

Where:

  • $$f_w$$ is the fractional flow of water.
  • $$k_{ro}$$ is the relative permeability of oil.
  • $$k_{rw}$$ is the relative permeability of water.
  • $$\mu_o$$ is the viscosity of oil.
  • $$\mu_w$$ is the viscosity of water.

Dykstra-Parsons Model

The Dykstra-Parsons model is used to estimate the vertical sweep efficiency in a layered reservoir. The Dykstra-Parsons coefficient, which characterizes the heterogeneity of the reservoir, is given by:

$$V_{DP} = \frac{k_{50} - k_{84.1}}{k_{50}}$$

Where:

  • $$V_{DP}$$ is the Dykstra-Parsons coefficient.
  • $$k_{50}$$ is the median permeability.
  • $$k_{84.1}$$ is the permeability at the 84.1 percentile.
